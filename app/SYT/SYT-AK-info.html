Goals

  • Improve outcomes for adolescents by implementing Parenting with Love and Limits ® (PLL) in two new communities.
  • Improve functional outcomes for transition-age youth by implementing the Transition to Independence Process in two new communities.
  • Develop and operationalize a standardized assessment tool to meet the application requirements, with the principal strategy of implementation that will involve:
    • Developing a standardized behavioral health assessment that includes a Level of Care/Acuity component, such as the Child and Adolescent Service Intensity Instrument (CASSI)
    • Aligning into the existing clinical practice of Treatment Plan Reviews (every 4 months)
    • Programming the standardized assessment and LOC instrument into the Alaska's Automated Information Management System (AKAIMS) application for electronic data entry and reporting
  • Reduce out-of-home and out-of-community placements for rural and Alaska Native youth and families by developing PLL and Treatment Improvement Protocols (TIP) implementation models in rural Alaska,
  • Ensure the long-term sustainability of PLL and TIP by developing an urban training hub and in-state trainers.

Population Served

Adolescents (ages 12−18) and transitional-age youth and young adults (ages 18−24) who experience substance abuse disorders or co-occurring substance abuse and mental health disorders; their families
