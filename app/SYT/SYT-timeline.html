  • August 2014

    • Vermont

      August 25–27, 2014

      The initiative has high-level support, and substance use disorders are receiving State attention. The project coordinator is developing a work plan to identify and implement upcoming tasks. The interagency council has invited key State agency stakeholders. A financial mapping workgroup has developed a financial mapping plan, collected preliminary aggregate data, and is disaggregating the data to identify expenditures specifically for adolescents and young adults. The initiative’s workforce development subcommittee includes representatives from the treatment community and from substance abuse, mental health, education, health, child welfare, juvenile justice, and Medicaid services State agencies. The two provider sites are implementing evidence-based practices that are well received by administrators and clinicians. The project’s experienced evaluator plans to develop a data workgroup and use data for decision-making and policy change.

  • September - October 2014

    • Alaska

      September 30–October 2, 2014

      The initiative’s high-level leadership and skilled, experienced staff have a history of successful cross-system collaboration. Its policy advisory council meets regularly and includes strong community-level and non-State agency representation. The team will continue to revise and update Alaska’s financial map to reflect cross-agency financial information. The team’s plan for dissemination of evidence-based practices includes training staff from multiple agencies. Several funding sources have been identified to support workforce development and dissemination activities. The project’s two experienced provider sites—serving a diverse population of youth and young adults referred from multiple sources—have high client retention rates. The project implementation team meets at least monthly and serves as a learning community.

    • Idaho

      October 21–23, 2014

      The principal investigator is invested in the project, and the coordinator is experienced with the young adult population. The team has a long collaborative working relationship with partner agencies. The project’s monitoring and advisory group (MAG), with broad representation of key agencies and partners serving the target population, meets at least bimonthly. Many MAG partners believe the SYT award aligns well with their respective agency strategic plans. The team is developing the Idaho financial map, reaching out to State agencies that may provide services for the population, and will continue revising and updating the financial map. The team has a well-thought-out plan for training related to their selected evidence-based practices. The team is interested in moving to a more person-centered, recovery-oriented approach and model of care. Advisory boards in both provider regions include diverse representation, meet regularly, and have high energy and engagement levels.

    • District of Columbia

      October 28–30, 2014

      Agency leadership has created an environment of progress and service evolution and is building on accomplishments and applying knowledge learned from previous Federal awards. The project team has a clearly articulated logic model and operations plan. The interagency council has a broad membership across relevant agencies, a history of interagency collaboration, and a productive working environment. The awardee has a cooperative and collaborative working relationship with Medicaid, previous exposure to financial mapping through other awards, and an executive committee that can influence data gathering for financial mapping. An experienced consultant assists with workforce development training, staff retention activities, implementation of evidence-based practices, fidelity, and referrals. The initiative’s four provider sites are culturally diverse, geographically representative of the District, dedicated to working with the population, and skilled in engaging youth.

  • December 2014

    • Mississippi

      December 9–11, 2014

      The experienced leadership team has a collaborative history, a vision for sustaining long-term system change post award, and awareness of major financing and reimbursement issues (e.g., Medicaid, use of Block Grant, and private insurance concerns). Site-level providers have a forum to interact and share information and resources with State administrators. Block grant funds are covering adolescent services. Staff have contacted many relevant agencies to obtain fiscal data, have received preliminary responses, and show good grasp of internal processes needed to begin financial mapping. The draft workforce development plan is clearly formatted and reflects numerous training activities, including GPRA data on trainings. The initiative’s provider sites are competent, dedicated, understand adolescent needs, and use the blending with wraparound model to provide comprehensive recovery support services.
