Goals

  • Develop and improve access to quality treatment for adolescents and transitional-aged youth with substance use and co-occurring mental health conditions and their families by implementing the evidence-based Adolescent Community Reinforcement Approach (A-CRA) and Assertive Continuing Care (ACC) and the Global Appraisal of Individual Needs (GAIN) assessment instrument, through two State-approved behavioral health agencies serving culturally and geographically diverse populations.
  • Implement a planning process that includes youth and families and project-related experiences from year 1 for disseminating the service approach statewide through various methods, including training centers, learning communities, and providers associations.
  • Develop policies and practices to improve outcomes for adolescent and transitional-aged youth as they move from youth to adult services systems.
  • Expand and strengthen the behavioral health workforce, including treatment providers, family advocates, and peer specialists.
  • Develop a financial map and implement a sustainable financing plan using Federal, State and third-party funding.

Population Served

Adolescents (ages 12−17) and transitional-aged youth (ages 18−25) with substance use disorders, including those with co-occurring mental health disorders
