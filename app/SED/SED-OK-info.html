Goals

  • Develop and improve access to quality treatment for adolescents with substance use and co-occurring mental disorders and their families.
  • Implement an evidence-based assessment that will enable a feedback loop for collaborating agencies to: (1) engage in process improvement; (2) identify and resolve gaps in services; and (3) measure client outcomes in real time.
  • In year 2, enter into a planning process that includes family and youth and draws from the experiences in year 1 to design the process for identifying additional providers to be trained in the Global Appraisal of Individual Needs (GAIN); by the end of year 2, commence training for additional provider organizations.
  • In year 2, enter into a planning process that includes family and adolescents and draws from the experiences in year 1 to design the process for identifying additional providers to be trained in the Adolescent Community Reinforcement Approach (A-CRA); by the beginning of year 3, commence training for additional provider organizations.
  • Develop Oklahoma code and funding strategies that support the infrastructure needed to sustain the evidence-based practices at the end of CSAT funding.

Population Served

Adolescents (ages 12–17) and their families
